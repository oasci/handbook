**Software development** <progress value="95" max="100" style="--value: 100; --max: 100;"></progress>

I owe my primary exposure to software development to the [computer club][cclub] at [Western Michigan University][wmu].
Python is my first&mdash;and primary&mdash;programming language.
Being self taught, I found that looking at mature Python projects was the best, albeit challenging and sometimes disheartening, way to learn.
Much of my growth was spending hours trying to understand from [cclib][cclib]; in fact, [my first pull request ever](https://github.com/cclib/cclib/pull/1034) was for [cclib][cclib].
[sGDML](https://github.com/stefanch/sGDML), [Ray](https://www.ray.io/), [NumPy](https://numpy.org/), [Sphinx](https://www.sphinx-doc.org/en/master/), [PySCF](https://github.com/pyscf/pyscf), and [Psi4](https://psicode.org/) were also tremendously important for my career.

Today, I value open-source software that is well-documented, user friendly, and maintainable.
